Neural networksA neural network is a classifier made up of a number of layers: The circles that you see in the graph are called artificial neurons. Neurons are arranged in layers. A network has three kinds of layers: The left-most layer is called the input layer. The right-most layer is called the output layer. The rest are called hidden layers. To make a decision, we pass the input at the left and obtain the output at the right. This is termed a forward pass The neurons across layers are connected by edges. Each edge has a weight associated with it. The weight denotes the strength of an edge.
